## 🧠 SHORT QUESTIONS (2–3 Marks)

### Q1. ⭐⭐⭐ Define a Product in marketing terms.

**Philip Kotler:** *"A product is anything that can be offered to a market for attention, acquisition, use or consumption that might satisfy a want or need."*

**William Stanton:** *"A product is a set of tangible and intangible attributes including packaging, colour, price and services which the buyer may accept as offering want satisfaction."*

**A product can be:** Physical goods | Services | Ideas | Persons | Places | Experiences

> 🧠 **Trick:** Product = **GSIPPE** → Goods, Services, Ideas, Persons, Places, Experiences

---

### Q2. ⭐⭐⭐ What are the 5 Levels of a Product (Kotler)?

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Core Product** | The fundamental benefit customer is really buying |
| 2 | **Actual Product** | Brand name, features, quality, design, packaging |
| 3 | **Expected Product** | Attributes customers normally expect (clean hotel room) |
| 4 | **Augmented Product** | Extra benefits — warranty, free delivery, after-sales service |
| 5 | **Potential Product** | All possible future improvements |

**Classic Example:** Customer buys a drill → Core benefit = **HOLES**, not the drill itself!

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"CAEAP"** = Core → Actual → Expected → Augmented → Potential

---

### Q3. ⭐⭐ Convenience Products vs Specialty Products?

| Aspect | Convenience Products | Specialty Products |
|---|---|---|
| **Buying Effort** | Minimal — bought routinely | Very high — special effort made |
| **Price** | Low | High (premium) |
| **Distribution** | Everywhere (Intensive) | Very few outlets (Exclusive) |
| **Examples** | Bread, milk, soap, biscuits | Rolex watch, BMW, designer wear |

---

### Q4. ⭐⭐ Define Product Mix.

**Product Mix** = The **complete set of all products and product lines** a company offers for sale.

**4 Dimensions:**
- **Width** — Number of different product lines (HUL: soaps, shampoos, detergents, foods = 4 lines)
- **Length** — Total number of items across all lines
- **Depth** — Variants within each line (Lux: Rose, Sandal, Aqua = 3 variants)
- **Consistency** — How closely related all product lines are

---

### Q5. ⭐⭐⭐ What is Brand Equity?

**Brand Equity** = The **extra value** a brand adds to a product beyond its functional benefits — the premium consumers are willing to pay because of the brand name.

**Example:** Nike shoes cost ₹5,000; similar unbranded shoes cost ₹500. The extra ₹4,500 = Nike's Brand Equity.

**David Aaker's 5 Components:**
1. Brand Awareness
2. Brand Loyalty
3. Perceived Quality
4. Brand Associations
5. Other Proprietary Assets (patents, trademarks)

---

### Q6. ⭐⭐ List the 3 Levels of Packaging.

| Level | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Immediate container holding the product | Toothpaste tube |
| **Secondary** | Outer wrapping protecting primary package | Cardboard box around tube |
| **Shipping** | Container for bulk transport/storage | Corrugated master box of 24 tubes |

---

### Q7. ⭐⭐ What is a Product Line? Give an example.

**Product Line** = A group of **closely related products** sold to the same customers, through the same channels, at similar prices.

**Example:** Colgate's toothpaste line — Regular, Sensitive, Total, Whitening, Kids — all toothpastes targeting slightly different needs.

**Key Decisions:** Line Stretching | Line Filling | Line Pruning | Line Modernisation

---

### Q8. ⭐⭐ Name 4 types of Product Support Services (After-Sales).

1. **Installation** — Setting up the product at the customer's location (e.g., AC installation)
2. **Warranty & Guarantee** — Free repair/replacement if product fails
3. **Maintenance & Repair** — Fixing defects after sale
4. **Technical Support / Helpline** — Call centres, online help, training

---

### Q9. ⭐⭐⭐ What is Branding? What are its advantages?

**Brand (AMA):** *"A name, term, sign, symbol, or design intended to identify the goods/services of one seller and differentiate them from competitors."*

**Brand Elements:** Brand Name | Brand Mark/Logo | Trademark | Trade Name

**Advantages for Sellers:**
1. Differentiation from competitors
2. Enables premium pricing
3. Builds customer loyalty
4. Legal protection (trademark)
5. Makes new product launches easier (brand extension)

**Advantages for Consumers:**
1. Easy product identification
2. Quality assurance — reduces purchase risk
3. Status and self-expression

---

### Q10. ⭐⭐ What is the Product Life Cycle? Name its 4 stages.

**PLC (Philip Kotler):** *"The course of a product's sales and profits over its lifetime."*

**4 Stages:**
1. **Introduction** — Low sales, negative profit, innovators, heavy advertising
2. **Growth** — Rising sales, rising profit, early majority, expanding distribution
3. **Maturity** — Peak sales, declining profit, late majority, intense competition
4. **Decline** — Falling sales, low profit, laggards, minimal promotion

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"I Grow More Daily"** (I=Introduction, G=Growth, M=Maturity, D=Decline)

## ✍️ LONG QUESTIONS (5 Marks)

### Q1. ⭐⭐⭐ Classify consumer products based on buying behaviour.

**4 Types:**

**1. 🛒 Convenience Products**
- Minimal buying effort | Low price | Bought frequently
- Intensive distribution (available everywhere)
- **Examples:** Bread, milk, soap, newspapers, matchboxes
- **Marketer Focus:** Maximum availability + brand recall

**2. 🏪 Shopping Products**
- Consumer compares price, quality, features before buying | Medium-High price
- Selective distribution
- **Examples:** Clothing, furniture, TV, laptop, appliances
- **Marketer Focus:** Clear product info for comparison; good store experience

**3. 💎 Specialty Products**
- Very high effort — buyer will NOT accept substitutes | High/premium price
- Exclusive distribution (very few outlets)
- **Examples:** Rolex, BMW, designer handbags, luxury perfumes
- **Marketer Focus:** Maintain exclusivity and premium image

**4. 📋 Unsought Products**
- Consumer does NOT normally think of buying | Varies in price
- Aggressive personal selling needed
- **Examples:** Life insurance, encyclopaedias, fire extinguishers, funeral services
- **Marketer Focus:** Create awareness first, then persuade

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"C-S-S-U"** → Convenience (cheap, easy), Shopping (compare), Specialty (exclusive), Unsought (not wanted)

---

### Q2. ⭐⭐⭐ Explain David Aaker's Model of Brand Equity.

**Brand Equity** = Extra value a product gets from its brand name.

**5 Components by David Aaker:**

**1. Brand Awareness**
- How widely the brand is known and remembered
- Levels: Unaware → Recognition → Recall → **Top-of-Mind**
- Example: "Think cola → Think Coca-Cola" = Top-of-mind awareness

**2. Brand Loyalty**
- Degree of repeat purchase and resistance to switching
- Loyal customers = cheaper to retain than acquiring new ones
- Example: Apple fans queue overnight for every new iPhone

**3. Perceived Quality**
- Consumer's overall perception of brand quality (even without direct testing)
- Allows premium pricing; builds trust
- Example: "Sony" is perceived as high-quality even without testing every product

**4. Brand Associations**
- Mental connections consumers have with the brand — images, feelings, values
- Example: Volvo → Safety | Nike → Performance | Amul → Taste of India

**5. Other Proprietary Assets**
- Patents, trademarks, exclusive channel relationships
- Create barriers to competition; protect market position

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"A-L-PQ-A-P"** → Awareness, Loyalty, Perceived Quality, Associations, Proprietary Assets

### Q3. ⭐⭐⭐ Explain the 4 Stages of the Product Life Cycle (PLC) with diagram.

**PLC = The course of a product's sales and profits over its entire lifetime.**

```
    SALES
      |             ____
      |           /      \
      |          /        \___________
      |    _____/  MATURITY   DECLINE
      |   /   GROWTH
      |  / INTRO
      |/_____________________________ TIME
```

**Stage 1: 🚀 Introduction**
- Sales: Low | Profit: Negative/Very Low | Customers: **Innovators**
- Competition: None/Little | Price: High or Low
- Goal: **Create Awareness + Encourage Trial**
- Example: EVs in India in early 2010s

**Stage 2: 📈 Growth**
- Sales: Rising fast | Profit: Increasing | Customers: **Early Majority**
- Competition: Growing | Distribution: Expanding
- Goal: **Maximise Market Share**
- Example: Smartphones in India 2010–2015

**Stage 3: 🏆 Maturity**
- Sales: Peak, then slowing | Profit: Declining | Customers: **Late Majority**
- Competition: Very Intense | Price Wars common
- Goal: **Defend Market Share + Maximise Profit**
- Strategies to extend: Market Modification | Product Modification | Mix Modification
- Example: Toothpaste, soft drinks, colour TV

**Stage 4: 📉 Decline**
- Sales: Falling sharply | Profit: Very low/Negative | Customers: **Laggards**
- Competition: Reducing (weak players exit)
- Goal: **Reduce costs + Milk the brand or discontinue**
- Options: Maintain | Harvest | Drop
- Example: Landline phones, fax machines, VCDs

**PLC Summary Table:**

| | Introduction | Growth | Maturity | Decline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sales | Low | Rising | Peak | Falling |
| Profit | Negative | Rising | High then falling | Very low |
| Customers | Innovators | Early Majority | Late Majority | Laggards |
| Competition | Low | Growing | Intense | Reducing |

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"I Grow More Daily"** (Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline)

### Q4. ⭐⭐⭐ Outline the 8 Stages of the New Product Development (NPD) Process.

**Why New Products?** Existing products age | Customer needs evolve | Competition forces innovation

**8 Stages:**

**Stage 1: 💡 Idea Generation**
- Sources: Internal (R&D, employees) + External (customers, competitors, market research)
- Techniques: Brainstorming, focus groups, surveys

**Stage 2: 🔍 Idea Screening**
- Filter ideas — keep good ones, reject bad ones
- **Drop Error** = Good idea rejected (costly!) | **Go Error** = Bad idea approved (more costly!)

**Stage 3: 📋 Concept Development & Testing**
- Develop detailed **product concept** from customer's perspective
- Test with target consumers: "Would you buy this? At what price?"

**Stage 4: 📊 Marketing Strategy Development**
- Define target market, positioning, sales goals, price, distribution, budget

**Stage 5: 💰 Business Analysis**
- Estimate sales, costs, break-even, profit
- If financially viable → move forward

**Stage 6: 🔧 Product Development (Prototype)**
- R&D builds the **physical prototype**
- ⚠️ **Most expensive stage** — huge investment; if it fails, loss is significant

**Stage 7: 🧪 Test Marketing**
- Launch in 1–2 representative markets
- Collect real sales data, fine-tune marketing mix
- **Advantage:** Reduces risk | **Disadvantage:** Time-consuming; alerts competitors

**Stage 8: 🚀 Commercialisation (Full Launch)**
- Full-scale national launch
- Decide: WHEN + WHERE + TO WHOM + HOW to launch

> 🧠 **Trick:** Count 1 to 8: **Idea → Screen → Concept → Strategy → Business → Prototype → Test → Launch**

### Q5. ⭐⭐ Describe Functions and Importance of Packaging and Labelling.

**PACKAGING = Designing and producing the container/wrapper for a product.**
> Philip Kotler: Called the **"Fifth P"** of marketing

**10 Functions of Packaging:**
1. **Protection** — from damage, spoilage, contamination
2. **Containment** — holds product together for handling
3. **Information** — communicates ingredients, usage, expiry
4. **Promotion** — acts as a **"Silent Salesman"** at point of sale
5. **Convenience** — easy-to-open, resealable, portable
6. **Differentiation** — stands out on crowded store shelves
7. **Facilitates Storage** — standardised packs for easy warehousing
8. **Enables Segmentation** — family packs vs single-use sachets
9. **Brand Building** — consistent packaging reinforces brand identity
10. **Eco-Positioning** — sustainable packaging = environmental responsibility

---

**LABELLING = Display of information on the product's package.**

**4 Types of Labels:**
1. **Brand Label** — Only brand name (fruit crates labelled "Amul")
2. **Grade Label** — Quality grade (Grade A eggs, BIS standards)
3. **Descriptive Label** — Usage, ingredients, handling info (most food labels)
4. **Informative Label** — Highly detailed info (pharmaceutical/medicine labels)

**Legal Requirements on Labels (India):**
- Manufacturer name & address | Net weight/volume | Ingredients list
- Date of manufacture & expiry | MRP (with taxes) | Batch number
