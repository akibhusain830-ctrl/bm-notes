### Q1. Discuss any TWO features of Marketing.

**1. Customer-Oriented**
- Modern marketing is entirely focused on the **customer** — understanding and satisfying their needs better than competitors.
- The customer is the centre of ALL marketing activity.

**2. Continuous Process**
- Marketing is not a one-time activity. It is a **continuous, ongoing process** — from identifying needs, creating products, promoting, distributing, and building long-term relationships.

*(Other features: Pervasive | Goal-Oriented | Exchange-Based | Creates Utility)*

### Q2. Explain the concept of Product Mix with suitable examples.

**Product Mix** = The **complete set of all products and product lines** a company offers for sale.

**4 Dimensions:**
- **Width** — Number of different product lines
- **Length** — Total items across all lines
- **Depth** — Variants within each line
- **Consistency** — How related all lines are

**Example:** HUL's product mix:
- Soaps line: Dove, Lux, Lifebuoy
- Detergents line: Surf Excel, Rin, Wheel
- Foods line: Knorr soups
→ Width = 3 lines | Depth of soaps = 3 variants

### Q3. State the difference between a Brand and a Trademark.

| Aspect | Brand | Trademark |
|---|---|---|
| **Meaning** | A name, term, symbol, or design that identifies a seller's product | The part of a brand given **legal protection** by registration |
| **Purpose** | Identifies and differentiates the product | Provides legal exclusive rights to the owner |
| **Registration** | Not necessarily registered | Registered with the government |
| **Example** | "Colgate" as a brand name | Colgate® — the ® symbol indicates trademark |

> 🧠 **Simple rule:** Every trademark is a brand, but NOT every brand is a trademark.

### Q4. What does Predatory Pricing mean?

**Predatory Pricing** = Setting prices **extremely low (sometimes below cost)** with the deliberate intention of **driving competitors out of the market**, then raising prices once they are eliminated.

- **Purpose:** Eliminate competition and establish monopoly
- **Example:** A large supermarket slashes prices of key items below cost to bankrupt nearby small shops
- **Legal Status:** Considered **anti-competitive and illegal** in many countries under competition law
- It is a form of **price war** used as a weapon rather than a legitimate business strategy

### Q5. Briefly discuss any TWO methods of Sales Promotion.

**1. Discounts and Price Reductions**
- Temporarily reducing the price of a product to encourage immediate purchase.
- Creates urgency and attracts price-sensitive buyers.
- Example: Flipkart Big Billion Day — 50% off on electronics.

**2. Free Samples**
- Distributing free product samples to potential customers to encourage trial.
- Especially effective for new products — removes the risk of trying something unknown.
- Example: Shampoo sachets distributed free in newspapers.

*(Other methods: Coupons | Buy-1-Get-1 Free | Contests | Loyalty Points | Cashback)*

### Q7. What is Social Marketing?

**Social Marketing** = The use of **marketing principles and techniques to change behaviour for social good** — promoting ideas, behaviours, or practices that benefit society.

**Key Point:** Social marketing sells **ideas and behaviour change**, NOT products for profit.

**Examples:**
- Anti-tobacco campaigns ("Smoking Kills")
- Road safety campaigns ("Don't drink and drive")
- COVID-19 vaccination drives
- Swachh Bharat (Clean India) campaign
- "Save Water" awareness campaigns

> 🧠 **Trick:** Social Marketing = "Marketing for the greater good of society"

### Q8. Discuss any TWO factors affecting the selection of Distribution Channel.

**1. Nature of the Product**
- Perishable products (milk, vegetables) need **short, direct channels** — minimum time from producer to consumer.
- Bulky/heavy products use fewer intermediaries to reduce handling costs.
- Technical/complex products (machinery) need **direct channels** with expert salespeople.
- FMCG products need **long, indirect channels** for wide reach.

**2. Nature of the Market / Target Customers**
- **Geographically concentrated market** → Direct or short channel is enough.
- **Widely dispersed market** (all over India) → Long indirect channel with wholesalers and retailers needed.
- **Industrial buyers** (fewer, larger orders) → Direct channel preferred.
- **Consumer market** (millions of small buyers) → Multiple intermediaries needed.

### Q1. Discuss the Five-Stage Model of Consumer Buying Decision Process.

**Stage 1: Need/Problem Recognition**
- Consumer realises a gap between current state and desired state.
- Triggered by internal stimuli (hunger, broken phone) or external stimuli (ad, friend's recommendation).
- **Marketer's role:** Trigger need recognition through advertising.

**Stage 2: Information Search**
- Consumer actively seeks information about solutions.
- **Internal search:** Memory, past experience.
- **External search:** Friends, internet, reviews, ads, salespeople.
- **Marketer's role:** Ensure brand is visible in all information sources.

**Stage 3: Evaluation of Alternatives**
- Consumer compares options based on criteria: price, quality, brand, features.
- Creates an **"evoked set"** — shortlist of acceptable brands.
- **Marketer's role:** Ensure your brand is on the shortlist and compares favourably.

**Stage 4: Purchase Decision**
- Consumer selects one brand and decides to buy.
- May be influenced last-minute by unexpected factors (friend's advice, in-store promotion, availability).
- **Marketer's role:** Remove all purchase barriers (easy payment, good availability).

**Stage 5: Post-Purchase Evaluation**
- Consumer compares actual performance vs expectations.
  - Performance ≥ Expectations → **Satisfied/Delighted**
  - Performance < Expectations → **Dissatisfied → Cognitive Dissonance (Buyer's Remorse)**
- **Marketer's role:** Provide excellent after-sales support; reassuring follow-up communication.

> 🧠 **Trick:** **NI-EPP** → Need → Info → Evaluate → Purchase Decision → Post-Purchase

### Q2. What are the Major Functions of a Wholesaler?

A **wholesaler** buys goods in large quantities from manufacturers and sells in smaller quantities to retailers.

**7 Major Functions:**

1. **Bulk Breaking** — Buys in large lots from manufacturers; breaks into smaller lots for retailers
2. **Warehousing and Storage** — Stores goods until retailers need them; reduces burden on manufacturers
3. **Transportation** — Arranges movement of goods from producers to retailers
4. **Financing** — Provides credit to retailers (buy now, pay later); also pays manufacturers in advance
5. **Risk Bearing** — Holds inventory; bears risk of price changes, damage, or unsold stock
6. **Market Information** — Collects and passes consumer demand information back to producers
7. **Promotion** — Assists in promoting products through displays and local advertising

### Q3. Explain the Concept of Channel of Distribution.

**Philip Kotler:** *"A distribution channel is a set of interdependent organisations that help make a product or service available for use or consumption by the consumer or business user."*

**Levels of Distribution Channel:**
```
Level 0 (Direct):  Producer → Consumer
Level 1:           Producer → Retailer → Consumer
Level 2:           Producer → Wholesaler → Retailer → Consumer
Level 3:           Producer → Agent → Wholesaler → Retailer → Consumer
```

**Importance:**
- Bridges geographical gap between producer and consumer
- Creates Place, Time, and Possession utility
- Reduces number of transactions needed
- Provides market information and after-sales service

**Types of Distribution Coverage:**
- **Intensive** — Everywhere (Coca-Cola, Colgate)
- **Selective** — Selected outlets (LG TVs)
- **Exclusive** — Very few outlets (Rolex, Rolls-Royce)

### Q4. Key Factors to Consider When Deciding on a Promotion Mix.

**1. Type of Product**
- Consumer goods → more Advertising; Industrial goods → more Personal Selling

**2. Target Market**
- Literacy level, media habits, geographic spread of the audience determine which tools are appropriate

**3. Stage of Product Life Cycle**
- Introduction → Heavy advertising to create awareness
- Growth → Build preference and brand loyalty
- Maturity → Sales promotions and reminder advertising
- Decline → Minimal promotion

**4. Budget Available**
- Limited budget → Cannot afford mass TV advertising; use cost-effective tools (social media, direct marketing)

**5. Push vs Pull Strategy**
- Push strategy → Focus on intermediaries (personal selling, trade promotions)
- Pull strategy → Focus on consumers (advertising, consumer promotions)

**6. Competitor's Promotion**
- Must respond to or differentiate from competitor promotional activities

**7. Nature of Market**
- B2B markets → Personal selling dominant
- B2C markets → Advertising and digital marketing dominant

### Q5. Write a Short Note on Green Marketing.

**Green Marketing** = The development, promotion, and distribution of products and services in a way that is **environmentally responsible and sustainable.**

**Also called:** Eco-marketing | Environmental marketing | Sustainable marketing

**Characteristics:**
- Products made using eco-friendly materials
- Environmentally safe manufacturing processes
- Recyclable or biodegradable packaging
- Energy-efficient products
- Honest environmental claims (no "greenwashing")

**Why it's important:**
- Growing consumer environmental consciousness
- Government regulations on pollution and packaging
- Competitive differentiation on sustainability
- Long-term cost savings through energy efficiency

**Examples:**
- Electric vehicles (Tesla, Tata Nexon EV)
- Bamboo toothbrushes, recycled paper products
- ITC's eco-friendly packaging
- Body Shop's cruelty-free, natural products

> 🧠 **Trick:** Green Marketing = "Market products that are GOOD for the planet"

### Q6. Briefly Discuss the Various Types of Pricing Strategies.

| Strategy | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Market Skimming** | High initial price → lower over time | New iPhone at ₹1,20,000 |
| **Penetration Pricing** | Very low initial price → gain market share | Jio launched at ₹0 |
| **Psychological Pricing** | Price to influence perception | ₹999 instead of ₹1,000 |
| **Premium/Prestige Pricing** | Permanently high price → signal luxury | Rolex, Mercedes, Louis Vuitton |
| **Bundle Pricing** | Multiple products at one combined price | McDonald's value meal |
| **Promotional Pricing** | Temporary price reduction | Diwali sales, flash sales |
| **Economy Pricing** | Bare minimum cost, bare minimum price | Generic medicines, budget airlines |
| **Discriminatory Pricing** | Different prices for different segments | Student ticket vs normal ticket at a movie |
| **Going-Rate Pricing** | Price at same level as major competitors | Petrol stations matching each other |
| **Cost-Plus Pricing** | Cost + fixed markup | Cost ₹100 + 30% = ₹130 |

### Q7. Describe the Benefits of Market Segmentation.

**Market Segmentation** = Dividing a large heterogeneous market into smaller homogeneous groups.

**Benefits:**

1. **Better Understanding of Customers** — Deep analysis of different groups reveals their needs, motivations, and behaviour.

2. **More Effective Marketing** — Tailored messages resonate better; higher conversion rates and response.

3. **Efficient Resource Use** — Concentrate resources on most profitable and accessible segments instead of wasting on everyone.

4. **Competitive Advantage** — Serve specific segments so well that competitors find it difficult to attack.

5. **Product Differentiation** — Develop different variants for different segments (Colgate Regular, Sensitive, Whitening).

6. **Identifies New Opportunities** — Reveals underserved or unserved segments representing new business potential.

7. **Improves Customer Satisfaction** — Tailored products and messages → customers feel understood → higher satisfaction.

8. **Facilitates Better Pricing** — Different price points for different segments (premium for high-income, economy for budget-conscious).

### Q8. Explain how the 4Ps of the Marketing Mix help in launching a Product in a New Market.

When launching a product in a new market, all 4Ps must be carefully designed together:

**P1 — PRODUCT**
- Define the **core benefit** the product provides to new market consumers.
- Adapt product features, packaging, and design to local preferences and culture.
- Example: McDonald's adapted its menu for India — McAloo Tikki, no beef.

**P2 — PRICE**
- For a new market: Use **Penetration Pricing** (low price to gain quick adoption) OR **Skimming** (high price for premium positioning).
- Consider local income levels, competitor prices, and perceived value.
- Example: Jio used ₹0 pricing to penetrate India's telecom market rapidly.

**P3 — PLACE (Distribution)**
- Design a **distribution channel** that efficiently reaches the new market's consumers.
- Decide: Intensive vs Selective vs Exclusive distribution based on product type.
- Build relationships with local wholesalers, retailers, and distributors.

**P4 — PROMOTION**
- Create **awareness** first — consumers don't know the product yet.
- Use advertising to inform + sales promotions (free samples, introductory offers) to encourage trial.
- Choose media channels that the new market's consumers actually use.

> **Key:** All 4Ps must be **mutually consistent** — a premium product needs premium price, selective distribution, and image-building advertising.

### Q1. Discuss the Components of Marketing Environment.

*(Covers both internal/micro and external/macro — see Part 1 notes for full PESTLE detail)*

**Marketing Environment** = All forces and actors inside and outside the company that affect marketing management's ability to build successful customer relationships.

**Philip Kotler:** *"A company's marketing environment consists of the actors and forces outside marketing that affect marketing management's ability to build and maintain successful relationships with target customers."*

**COMPONENT A: MICRO ENVIRONMENT (Near/Task Environment)**
Factors the company has some influence over:

| Actor | Role in Marketing |
|---|---|
| **The Company (Internal)** | Top management, finance, R&D, production, HR — all must align with marketing |
| **Suppliers** | Provide raw materials; poor suppliers = poor quality = unhappy customers |
| **Marketing Intermediaries** | Wholesalers, retailers, logistics firms — help distribute and promote products |
| **Customers** | The entire purpose of marketing; 5 types: Consumer, Business, Reseller, Govt, International |
| **Competitors** | Must monitor rivals' strategies; company must offer better value |
| **Publics** | Financial, media, government, citizen-action, internal, local publics |

**COMPONENT B: MACRO ENVIRONMENT (Far/External — PESTLE)**
Largely uncontrollable; must be monitored and adapted to:

| Force | Key Factors | Marketing Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Political/Legal** | Tax laws, trade policy, ad regulations | Regulations restrict content; import duties raise costs |
| **Economic** | GDP, inflation, interest rates, unemployment | Recession → price-sensitive buyers |
| **Social-Cultural** | Demographics, lifestyle, health trends | Health consciousness → organic food market boom |
| **Technological** | AI, e-commerce, 5G, data analytics | E-commerce opens new distribution channels |
| **Legal/Ecological** | Climate, green regulations, sustainability | Banning plastics → companies redesign packaging |
| **Competitive** | Porter's 5 Forces | Jio's entry disrupted entire telecom industry |

**How Environment Influences Marketing Decisions:**
- **Opportunities:** New technologies, growing segments, favourable regulations → company can exploit these
- **Threats:** New competitors, economic downturn, unfavourable laws → company must counter these
- **Adaptation:** Company adjusts Product, Price, Place, Promotion based on environmental changes

### Q2. What is Product Life Cycle? Describe its Various Stages.

**PLC Definition (Philip Kotler):** *"The product life cycle is the course of a product's sales and profits over its lifetime."*

Like a living being — a product is born, grows, matures, and eventually dies.

```
SALES/PROFIT
    |           ______
    |          /      \
    |         / MATURITY\____________
    |        /             DECLINE
    |  _____/ GROWTH
    | / INTRO
    |/_________________________ TIME
```

**Stage 1: 🚀 INTRODUCTION**
- **Sales:** Low (product is unknown)
- **Profit:** Negative or very low (high launch costs)
- **Customers:** Innovators (love trying new things)
- **Competition:** None or little
- **Marketing Goal:** Create awareness + encourage trial
- **Strategy:** Heavy advertising, select distribution, skimming or penetration pricing
- **Example:** EVs in India in early 2010s

**Stage 2: 📈 GROWTH**
- **Sales:** Rapidly rising
- **Profit:** Increasing (costs spread over higher volume)
- **Customers:** Early majority entering market
- **Competition:** Growing (attracted by profits)
- **Marketing Goal:** Maximise market share
- **Strategy:** Expand distribution, build brand preference, maintain/slightly reduce price
- **Example:** Smartphones in India 2010–2015; OTT platforms 2018–2020

**Stage 3: 🏆 MATURITY**
- **Sales:** Peak, then slowing (market saturated)
- **Profit:** Declining (intense price competition)
- **Customers:** Late majority (most potential buyers already using it)
- **Competition:** Very intense; price wars common
- **Marketing Goal:** Defend market share + maximise profit
- **Strategies to extend:** Market Modification | Product Modification | Marketing Mix Modification
- **Example:** Toothpaste, soft drinks, colour TVs

**Stage 4: 📉 DECLINE**
- **Sales:** Falling sharply
- **Profit:** Very low or negative
- **Customers:** Laggards (only most loyal remain)
- **Competition:** Reducing (weak players exit)
- **Marketing Goal:** Cut costs and milk or discontinue brand
- **Options:** Maintain | Harvest | Drop
- **Example:** Landline phones, fax machines, VCDs, black-and-white TVs

**Summary Table:**

| | Introduction | Growth | Maturity | Decline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sales | Low | Rising fast | Peak | Falling |
| Profit | Negative | Rising | High→Falling | Very low |
| Customers | Innovators | Early Majority | Late Majority | Laggards |
| Competition | Low | Growing | Intense | Reducing |

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"I Grow More Daily"** — Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline

### Q3. Highlight Objectives of Promotion. Differentiate Advertising vs Personal Selling.

**OBJECTIVES OF PROMOTION:**

1. **Inform** — Create awareness about new products, price changes, features
2. **Persuade** — Convince customers to prefer and purchase the brand
3. **Remind** — Keep brand top-of-mind; remind customers to repurchase
4. **Reinforce** — Reassure existing customers they made the right choice
5. **Differentiate** — Distinguish product from competing brands
6. **Build Brand Image** — Create strong, positive brand identity
7. **Generate Demand** — Stimulate primary and selective demand
8. **Stabilise Sales** — Smooth demand during off-peak seasons

---

**ADVERTISING vs PERSONAL SELLING:**

| Aspect | Advertising | Personal Selling |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Non-personal, mass communication | Personal, face-to-face communication |
| **Direction** | One-way (marketer → consumer) | Two-way (interactive) |
| **Flexibility** | Same message to everyone | Message tailored to each prospect |
| **Feedback** | Delayed (sales data, surveys) | Immediate (face-to-face reaction) |
| **Cost** | High total cost; low cost per contact | Lower total cost; high cost per contact |
| **Reach** | Mass audience | Limited — one-to-one |
| **Persuasion** | Less persuasive | Most persuasive promotional tool |
| **Best for** | Consumer goods; brand building | Industrial goods; high-value complex products |
| **Example** | TV commercial, Google ad | Insurance agent, pharmaceutical MR, car salesperson |

### Q4. Explain Target Marketing. Discuss Factors Influencing Consumer Buying Behaviour.

**TARGET MARKETING:**
Target marketing = Evaluating market segments and selecting which segment(s) to enter and serve.

**3-Step Process (STP):**
- **Segment** → Divide market into groups (Geographic, Demographic, Psychographic, Behavioural)
- **Target** → Evaluate and select the best segment(s)
- **Position** → Design product and image to occupy a distinctive place in target's mind

**4 Targeting Strategies:**
1. Undifferentiated (Mass) — one product for all
2. Differentiated — different products for different segments
3. Concentrated (Niche) — one segment served very well
4. Micromarketing — tailored to individuals or very small groups

---

**FACTORS INFLUENCING CONSUMER BUYING BEHAVIOUR:**

**A. CULTURAL FACTORS** *(Most Fundamental)*
1. **Culture** — Basic values, perceptions, wants learned from family and society
   - Example: McDonald's doesn't serve beef in India; offers McAloo Tikki — cultural adaptation
2. **Subculture** — Nationality, religion, region, age group
   - Example: South Indian food preferences differ from North Indian
3. **Social Class** — Upper, middle, lower — influences product choices, brands, media

**B. SOCIAL FACTORS**
1. **Reference Groups** — Aspirational groups (celebrities) → celebrity endorsements
2. **Family** — Most important reference group; influences household purchases
3. **Roles and Status** — Senior manager buys a BMW to signal professional status

**C. PERSONAL FACTORS**
1. **Age and Life Cycle** — Infants need baby food; teens want fashion; seniors need healthcare
2. **Occupation and Income** — Blue-collar vs white-collar different product preferences
3. **Lifestyle** — Health-conscious person buys organic food, gym memberships
4. **Personality** — Adventurous person prefers Red Bull; Royal Enfield

**D. PSYCHOLOGICAL FACTORS**
1. **Motivation (Maslow's Hierarchy)**
   - Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-Actualisation
   - Example: Someone fulfilling esteem needs buys a luxury watch
2. **Perception** — How people select, organise, interpret information
   - Selective Attention | Selective Distortion | Selective Retention
3. **Learning** — Experience changes behaviour; loyalty programmes use operant conditioning
4. **Beliefs and Attitudes** — "Brand X laptops are reliable" (belief) drives purchase

> 🧠 **Trick for 4 factors:** **"CSPP"** → Cultural, Social, Personal, Psychological
