## 🧠 SHORT QUESTIONS (2–3 Marks)

### Q1. ⭐⭐⭐ Define Marketing (Kotler).
**Philip Kotler:** *"Marketing is a social and managerial process by which individuals and groups obtain what they need and want through **creating, offering and exchanging** products of value with others."*

**AMA:** *"Marketing is the activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value."*

> 🧠 **Trick:** Kotler's 3 action words = **Create → Offer → Exchange**

---

### Q2. ⭐⭐⭐ Difference between Needs, Wants, and Demands?

| Concept | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Need** | Basic human requirement. Marketers do NOT create needs. | Food, shelter |
| **Want** | Specific way to satisfy a need. Culture & personality shape it. | Need food → Want pizza |
| **Demand** | Want + **Purchasing Power** (ability + willingness to buy) | Want BMW → Demand only if you can afford it |

> 🧠 **Trick:** **N→W→D** = Need (basic) → Want (specific) → Demand (affordable want)

### Q6. ⭐⭐ What is Cognitive Dissonance?

- **Definition:** Doubt/discomfort a buyer feels **after purchase** — "Did I make the right choice?"
- Also called: **Buyer's Remorse**
- **Occurs when:** Purchase was expensive, irreversible, or unchosen option looked good
- **Example:** After buying Samsung, you think "Should I have bought iPhone?" = Cognitive Dissonance

**How marketers reduce it:**
- After-sales calls & support
- Reassuring ads ("You made the right choice!")
- Good warranty & customer service

**Philip Kotler:** *"Positioning is designing the offering and image to occupy a **distinctive place** in the target market's mind."*

| Brand | Owns This Word |
|---|---|
| Volvo | Safety |
| Colgate | Cavity Protection |
| Nike | Performance |
| Amul | Taste of India |

> 🧠 **Trick:** Positioning = **"Owning a word in the customer's mind"**

### Q9. ⭐⭐⭐ What is Marketing Myopia? Give an example.

- **Concept by:** Theodore Levitt
- **Meaning:** Companies define their business **too narrowly around the product** instead of the customer's real need
- **Result:** They miss big trends and lose customers

**Classic Example:**
- Railroad companies saw themselves in the **"railroad business"** not the **"transportation business"**
- When cars and airlines arrived → they failed to adapt and lost customers

> 🧠 **Trick:** Myopia = **Shortsightedness** — so focused on the product, they missed the customer's real need

## ✍️ LONG QUESTIONS (5 Marks)

### Q1. ⭐⭐⭐ Explain the 5 Eras of Marketing Evolution.

**Era 1: 🏭 Production Era (Pre-1920s)**
- **Belief:** "If we make it, they'll buy it"
- **Focus:** Maximise production + reduce costs
- **Why:** Supply < Demand — everything got sold
- **Limitation:** Ignored what customers actually wanted
- **Example:** Henry Ford — "Any colour as long as it's black"

**Era 2: 🛍️ Product Era (1920s–30s)**
- **Belief:** "Best quality product automatically sells"
- **Focus:** Product improvement & engineering
- **Limitation:** **Marketing Myopia** — too focused on product, missed customer need
- **Example:** Buggy whip makers improving product as customers switched to cars

**Era 3: 💼 Sales Era (1930s–50s)**
- **Belief:** "Push and persuade customers to buy"
- **Why:** Great Depression → surplus goods, too few buyers
- **Focus:** Aggressive selling, advertising, promotions
- **Limitation:** Damaged trust; no repeat business
- **Example:** High-pressure door-to-door salespeople

**Era 4: 🎯 Marketing Era (1950s–90s)**
- **Belief:** "Find what customers want, then make it"
- **Big Shift:** "Sell what we make" → **"Make what we can sell"**
- **3 Pillars:** Customer Orientation | Integrated Marketing | Profitability
- **Example:** P&G does consumer research BEFORE developing products

**Era 5: 🌍 Societal Era (1990s–Now)**
- **Belief:** "Satisfy customers AND care for society AND planet"
- **Balances:** Customer Wants + Company Profits + Society's Welfare
- **Includes:** CSR, Green Marketing, Digital Marketing, Relationship Marketing
- **Example:** Tata's CSR | Eco-friendly packaging companies

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"People Prefer Selling More Sensibly"** (P→P→S→M→S)

### Q2. ⭐⭐⭐ Explain the 4Ps of Marketing Mix with examples.

**Definition (Philip Kotler):** *"Marketing mix is the set of marketing tools that the firm uses to pursue its marketing objectives in the target market."*
- Term coined by **Neil Borden** (1964) | 4Ps introduced by **E. Jerome McCarthy** (1960)

**P1 — PRODUCT** (What you offer)
- Anything offered to satisfy a need: goods, services, ideas, experiences
- **5 Levels:** Core Benefit → Actual → Expected → Augmented → Potential
- **Example:** Customer buys a drill → Core benefit = **HOLES** (not the drill!)

**P2 — PRICE** (What you charge)
- **ONLY revenue-generating** element of the 4Ps (others generate costs)
- Price signals positioning — high = premium; low = value
- **Example:** iPhone at ₹79,000 = premium/skimming pricing

**P3 — PLACE** (Where/how it reaches customers)
- All activities making the product available to customers
- **Levels:** L0 (Direct) | L1 (+ Retailer) | L2 (+ Wholesaler) | L3 (+ Agent)
- **Coverage:** Intensive | Selective | Exclusive
- **Example:** Coca-Cola = intensive (in every kirana, restaurant, vending machine)

**P4 — PROMOTION** (How you communicate)
- Inform, persuade, remind customers
- **6 Tools:** Advertising | Sales Promotion | Personal Selling | PR | Direct Marketing | Digital
- **Example:** Amul cartoon ads = PR | Flipkart sale = Sales Promotion

**7Ps for Services** → Add: People | Process | Physical Evidence

> 🧠 **Trick:** 4Ps = "What? (Product) How Much? (Price) Where? (Place) How Tell? (Promotion)"

### Q3. ⭐⭐ Explain the PESTLE framework (Macro Environment).

| Letter | Force | Key Factors | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| P | Political/Legal | Tax laws, trade policy, ad regulations | India banning single-use plastics |
| E | Economic | GDP, inflation, interest rates, unemployment | Recession → buyers become price-sensitive |
| S | Social-Cultural | Demographics, lifestyle, health trends | Health consciousness → organic food boom |
| T | Technological | AI, e-commerce, 5G, data analytics | Jio's 4G disrupted India's telecom industry |
| L | Legal/Ecological | Climate change, green regulations | Companies redesign packaging to reduce waste |
| E | Competitive | Porter's 5 Forces — rivals, substitutes | Jio entering telecom forced all others to cut prices |

> 🧠 **Trick:** PESTLE = 6 big uncontrollable forces. Monitor → Adapt → Survive.

### Q4. ⭐⭐ Describe the 6-Stage Consumer Decision Process.

| Stage | What Happens | Marketer's Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1. **Need Recognition** | Consumer realizes a gap between current & desired state | Trigger need via ads |
| 2. **Information Search** | Searches internally (memory) + externally (Google, friends, ads) | Be visible everywhere |
| 3. **Evaluation of Alternatives** | Compares brands on price, quality, features; creates "evoked set" | Be on the shortlist |
| 4. **Purchase Decision** | Selects one brand | Remove purchase barriers |
| 5. **Purchase Behaviour** | Actual buying act | Make buying smooth |
| 6. **Post-Purchase Evaluation** | Performance vs. Expectations. If performance < expectations → Cognitive Dissonance | Provide great after-sales support |

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"NI-EDP-P"** → Need, Info, Evaluate, Decide, Purchase, Post-purchase

---

### Q5. ⭐⭐ Explain the 4 Bases of Market Segmentation.

**Market Segmentation (Kotler):** *"Dividing a market into distinct groups of buyers who have different needs, characteristics, or behaviour."*

**1. Geographic** — Based on location (region, climate, city size)
- Example: Raincoat brand targets Northeast India

**2. Demographic** *(Most Widely Used)* — Age, gender, income, education, religion
- Example: Johnson's Baby → infants | BMW → high-income

**3. Psychographic** — Lifestyle, personality, values, interests
- Example: Red Bull → adventure-seekers | Organic brands → eco-conscious
- Tool: **VALS Framework**

**4. Behavioural** — Occasion, benefits sought, loyalty, usage rate
- **80-20 Rule:** 80% revenue from 20% of customers (heavy users)
- Example: Colgate — different variants for different benefit-seekers

> 🧠 **Trick:** **"GD-PB"** — Geographic, Demographic (easy/measurable), Psychographic, Behavioural (deep/specific)
